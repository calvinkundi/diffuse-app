Privacy Policy

Diffuse — Your companion for overwhelming moments

Last updated: May 2026 · Previous versions: April 2026, February 2026

The Simple Version

Your privacy matters — especially in a mental health app. Here's the short version:

  • Most of your data stays on your device and never leaves it
  • AI features are opt-in — if you enable them and request a reflection, your writing is sent to an AI service for processing
  • Voice-to-text — if you use the microphone to dictate instead of type, your voice is processed by your device's built-in speech recognition service (Apple or Google)
  • Crash reports are collected automatically so we can fix bugs, but they contain no personal content
  • We don't have accounts, and we don't use behavioural tracking, advertising, or analytics

The rest of this page explains each part in detail.

Data That Stays On Your Device

The following data is stored locally on your device and never sent anywhere:

  • Your app preferences and settings (dark mode, sound, haptic intensity, breathing pattern)
  • Session history (completion count, streaks, timestamps)
  • Brain dump journal entries you choose to save
  • Stress level check-in responses
  • Emotion history from your sessions

This data is stored using your device's secure storage (encrypted) and shared preferences. You can delete all of it at any time through the Settings page, or by uninstalling the app.

AI-Powered Features (Opt-In)

Diffuse includes optional AI-powered features that are off by default and only activate if you explicitly enable them. These include:

  • Brain dump reflections — a compassionate response to what you wrote
  • Reframe questions — personalised questions that gently challenge unhelpful thinking patterns
  • Pattern insights — observations about your emotional patterns across multiple sessions (available after 5+ sessions)

How your data is processed

When you use an AI feature, your data takes this path:

  1. Your data is sent from your device to a Firebase Cloud Function hosted on Google Cloud
  2. The Cloud Function forwards it to Anthropic's Claude AI for processing
  3. The AI response is returned to your device

Diffuse itself does not retain your data after the response is delivered. The third-party processors involved — Google Cloud and Anthropic — may briefly retain inputs for operational reliability and abuse prevention as described in their privacy policies. Anthropic's standard policy retains API inputs for up to 30 days for trust and safety review; we do not currently have a zero-retention arrangement in place. Neither provider uses your inputs to train their models. Your data is not associated with you or any account, because Diffuse does not use accounts.

When your device sends data to the Cloud Function, your IP address is incidentally visible to Google Cloud's request logs as part of standard internet routing, and is retained briefly under Google's default logging policies. Diffuse does not link your IP address to your activity within the app or to any other data we hold.

What is sent for brain dump reflections

  • Your brain dump text (up to 2,000 characters)
  • The emotional tone detected from your writing (e.g. "anxious", "sad")
  • The emotional arc of your writing session (how your emotions shifted as you wrote)
  • Emotionally significant phrases from your writing
  • Word count and writing duration
  • Whether the entry was written late at night

What is sent for reframe questions

  • Your brain dump text (up to 1,500 characters)
  • The emotional tone detected from your writing
  • Emotionally significant phrases from your writing
  • Keywords extracted from your writing

What is sent for pattern insights

  • A summary of your emotional patterns across sessions (e.g. "anxious — Tuesday 10pm, sad — Wednesday 8pm")
  • The number of sessions completed
  • Your actual journal text is not sent for pattern insights — only the detected emotions and timestamps

What is NOT sent

  • Your name, email, phone number, or any account information (there are no accounts)
  • Your location
  • Your device identifiers
  • Your saved journal history — only the current session's data, and only when you explicitly request it

Crisis content

If your writing contains language indicating a mental health crisis or self-harm — for example, phrases referring to suicide, self-harm, or hopelessness — Diffuse detects this locally on your device and:

  • Displays crisis support resources for your country
  • Does not send the content to the AI for reflection or reframing, even if AI features are enabled

This detection runs entirely on your device. No additional data is collected or transmitted as a result.

If you don't enable AI features

If you leave AI features disabled (the default), no writing or emotional data ever leaves your device. The app works fully without AI features.

Voice-to-Text (Speech Recognition)

The brain dump feature allows you to speak instead of type. If you use this feature:

  • Your voice audio is processed by your device's built-in speech recognition service — Apple's Speech Recognition on iOS or Google's Speech Recognition on Android
  • This processing may involve sending your audio to Apple or Google servers, depending on your device model and settings
  • Diffuse does not send your audio anywhere itself — it uses your device's standard speech-to-text capability
  • The converted text is stored locally on your device like any other brain dump entry

For details on how your voice data is handled, see Apple's Privacy Policy or Google's Privacy Policy.

Crash Reports (Firebase Crashlytics)

Diffuse uses Firebase Crashlytics to automatically collect crash reports when the app encounters an error. This helps us identify and fix bugs quickly.

What crash reports contain

  • Technical information about the crash (error type, stack trace, which screen the crash occurred on)
  • Device information (device model, operating system version, app version)
  • A random installation identifier (not linked to your identity)

What crash reports do NOT contain

  • Any of your journal entries or personal writing
  • Your name, email, or any personal information
  • The content you were viewing when the crash occurred

How long crash reports are kept

Firebase Crashlytics retains crash report data for approximately 90 days, after which it is automatically deleted. You can review Firebase's privacy information for details.

Sharing and Exporting Your Data

Diffuse allows you to export and share your journal entries:

  • Journal export: You can export your saved brain dump entries as a text file via your device's share sheet (e.g. to email, messaging apps, or cloud storage)
  • App sharing: You can share a link to the app with others via your device's share sheet

These actions are entirely initiated by you. Diffuse does not automatically share any of your data. Once data leaves the app via the share sheet, it is handled by whatever app or service you choose to share it with.

No Accounts Required

Diffuse does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide any personal information to use the app. There is no registration process.

Network Communication

Diffuse communicates with external servers only in these specific situations:

  • AI features (opt-in, user-initiated): When you explicitly request an AI reflection, reframe questions, or pattern insight, session data is sent to Google Cloud and Anthropic for processing
  • Voice-to-text (user-initiated): When you use the microphone to dictate, your voice may be sent to Apple or Google for speech recognition
  • Crash reports (automatic): If the app crashes, a technical error report is sent to Firebase Crashlytics
  • Crisis helpline links: Tapping a crisis number opens your phone's dialler or messaging app
  • Email and share links: Opens your device's email or share sheet
  • Privacy Policy and Terms links: Opens your browser to this page

The app does not perform any background network activity, behavioural tracking, or analytics beyond the above.

Third-Party Services

Diffuse uses the following third-party services:

  • Firebase Crashlytics (Google) — for crash reporting. Privacy info
  • Firebase Cloud Functions (Google) — as a secure relay for AI feature requests. Data passes through but is not stored by Diffuse. Privacy info
  • Anthropic Claude API — for AI-powered reflections, reframing, and pattern insights (opt-in only). Anthropic's API usage policy states that API inputs are not used to train models. Privacy info
  • Apple Speech Recognition / Google Speech Recognition — for voice-to-text dictation in the brain dump feature (user-initiated only). See Apple or Google privacy policies

Diffuse does not use any advertising, analytics, or tracking services.

Data Storage & Security

Local data is stored on your device using:

  • Flutter Secure Storage (encrypted) — for sensitive data like journal entries
  • Shared Preferences — for app settings and non-sensitive preferences

Data sent to the AI service is transmitted over HTTPS (encrypted in transit) and passes through Google Cloud and Anthropic's servers for processing. Diffuse does not retain this data on its own servers after processing; retention by the underlying providers is governed by their respective policies, linked above.

You can delete all locally stored data at any time through the app's Settings page using "Clear Progress" or "Clear All Data". Uninstalling the app will also remove all local data.

Children's Privacy

Diffuse is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16. If you believe a child under 16 has used the app, please contact us at the email address below and we will take appropriate action.

Mental Health Disclaimer

Diffuse is a wellness tool designed to help manage everyday stress and anxiety. It is not a medical device, does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment, and is not a substitute for professional mental health care.

The AI-powered features do not provide therapy, counselling, or clinical advice. They offer reflective responses and cognitive reframing questions only, and should not be treated as professional guidance.

If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, thoughts of self-harm, or suicidal ideation, please contact your local emergency services or a crisis helpline immediately. Crisis resources are available within the app.

International Users

Diffuse is available worldwide. If you enable AI features, your data may be processed on servers located outside your country. The Firebase Cloud Function and Anthropic's AI servers are primarily located in the United States. By enabling AI features, you consent to this transfer. If you do not enable AI features, no personal data leaves your device (except crash reports, which are processed by Google).

Rights for EU, UK, and EEA Users

If you are located in the European Union, United Kingdom, or European Economic Area, you have specific rights under the GDPR (and UK GDPR):

  • Right of access — to know what personal data is processed about you
  • Right to rectification — to correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — to have your data deleted
  • Right to restrict processing — to limit how your data is used
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a portable format
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority

Legal basis for processing. AI feature processing is based on your explicit consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR), which you can withdraw at any time by disabling AI features in Settings. Crash report processing is based on our legitimate interest in maintaining a stable, working app (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR). Voice-to-text processing is initiated by your action and handled by your device's operating system; the legal basis (and data controller) for that processing is Apple or Google, as applicable.

Special category data. When you use AI features, the content you share may include information about your mental and emotional state, which qualifies as "data concerning health" under Article 9 of the GDPR. Processing of such special-category data requires explicit consent in addition to the standard legal basis. Enabling AI features in Settings constitutes your explicit consent under Article 9(2)(a) GDPR. You can withdraw it at any time by disabling AI features, after which no further special-category data will be sent.

Because Diffuse stores almost all data locally on your device and does not collect personal identifiers, exercising most of these rights amounts to using "Clear All Data" in Settings or uninstalling the app. For questions about data processed by third-party services, contact us at the email below.

Your Rights (General)

Because Diffuse stores almost all data locally on your device, you have direct control over it:

  • Access: Your data is visible to you within the app at all times
  • Deletion: Use "Clear All Data" in Settings, or uninstall the app
  • Opt out of AI: Disable AI features in Settings at any time — no data will be sent for AI processing
  • Export: You can export your journal entries via the share sheet

If you have questions about data processed by our third-party services, please contact us and we will assist you.
